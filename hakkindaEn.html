About Us and Terms


About Us

The primary goals of the competition are to strengthen the artistic ties of students, to connect with national-international music schools and teachers, to create an environment for sharing, to discover new talents in piano performance, to promote classical piano music as it is one of the important components for the cultural education of the rising generation, and to encourage talented pianists who are at the beginning of their careers. In this competition, participants will be given the opportunity to take the stage, challenge themselves, gain experience in a platform other than their own environment, and experience the enthusiasm and passion of the amateur spirit.

Hisar Okulları 1. Uluslararası Piyano Yarışma Şartname

  1. The finalists of each category in the competition will bring their performances to life on stage at Hisar School from June 19 to June 21, 2017.
  2. The competition is open to all pianists from the age of 6 to 18, but excludes full-time students studying at the state conservatory.
  3. All candidates of all nationalities must fill out the application form on the official website of the competition, and upload their mp4 video from the given link by 23:59 on April 30, 2017. Participants are required to scan and email an ID card, certificate of school enrollment, and bank payment receipt.
  4. If any of the required documents are not submitted, the application will not be taken into account.
  5. There are 5 categories in the competition: (Regarding to the age of student in the date of competition):
    • Category A: 6-7 years old
    • Category B: 8-9 years old
    • Category C: 10-12 years old
    • Category D: 13-15 years old
    • Category E: 16-18 years old
  6. Fifteen candidates will be selected from each category to compete in the finals.
  7. In the finals, candidates from each category will perform live at Hisar school to an open public from June 19 to June 21, 2017.
  8. Candidates who compete in the finals must also attend the award ceremony.
  9. Participation fees for the competition are as follows:
    • Category A: 150 TL
    • Category B: 175 TL
    • Category C: 200 TL
    • Category D: 225 TL
    • Category E: 250 TL
    All participants must indicate which piece they will perform when filling out the application form. The preliminary selection will be done by a video recording. Pieces used for video recordings may also be played at the competition. Participants are free to choose the piece for the video recording. Finalists will perform the piece they have indicated in the application form at the finals. The video recordings cannot exceed the durations given below:
    • Category A: 1 minute
    • Category B: 2 minutes
    • Category C: 3 minutes
    • Category D: 4 minutes
    • Category E: 5 minutes
  10. Participants can send only one video recording for the preliminary selection. If participants send more than one video, the first video will be taken into consideration and the others will be disregarded.
  11. The pieces to be performed at the competitions do not need to be memorized.
  12. The jury’s decision is the final decision and will not be altered in any condition.
  13. Participants who advance to the final round must take stage at the stated date and time. If not, they will not be given another chance to participate.
  14. By applying to the competition, it is accepted that participants consent that the competition committee may take audio and video recordings and broadcast them for promotional purposes. All videos and photographs taken at the competition are the property of Hisar School. The publishing rights/copyrights of these materials are owned by Hisar School and can be used in the printed, digital, and social media of the school.
  15. Participants are responsible for paying their own travel and accommodation expenses.
  16. Once participants apply for the competition, they accept and agree to all the rules.
  17. Participants are free to choose their music piece. If one advances to the finals, s/he must perform the piece indicated in his/her application form. If one performs longer than the durations give below, his/her performance may be interrupted.
    • Category A: 2 minutes
    • Category B: 4 minutes
    • Category C: 6 minutes
    • Category D: 8 minutes
    • Category E: 11 minutes
  18. Awards for each category will be based on the following scoring system.
    • 95-100 score: First place certificate
    • 90-94 score: Second place certificate
    • 85-89 score: Third place certificate
    • 80-84 score: Fourth place certificate
    • Participants who score 79 or below will receive a participation certificate.

    Participants who score the highest in their categories will be awarded first place for their respective category and win prize money of 1000 TL.

    The jury will decide and present the Jury’s Special Award to one of the 5 first place winners, who will win an acoustic piano.
